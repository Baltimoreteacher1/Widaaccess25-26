What do these numbers mean?

            
A teacher-facing guide for reading the dashboard without a statistics background.

            
🌡️
Think of ACCESS like a snapshot
ACCESS scores show English language proficiency at one point in time. Growth shows how much the score changed. The target is the personalized goal. The gap tells you how far the student was from that goal.

            
Four numbers matter most

            

              Growth = Current score − Prior score

              Gap = Current score − Target score

              Met target? = Yes if Gap ≥ 0

              Near miss = Missed by 0.2 points or less
            

            
Important: ACCESS measures English language proficiency, not intelligence, motivation, or academic ability. Use the numbers to ask better questions, not to label students.

Growth and Gap

            
Growth answers “did they move?” Gap answers “did they reach the goal?”

            

              
📈
Growth
How much the ACCESS score changed from last year. Positive growth means the student moved up. Negative growth means the score declined.

              
🎯
Gap
How far the student was from the target. A gap of −0.1 is a near miss. A gap of −1.0 needs sustained support.

            

            
Declined students need barrier review first. Ask what changed before adding more practice: attendance, testing conditions, confidence, engagement, or a specific language-domain need.

          

        


        

          

            
Confidence range

            
A confidence range tells you how much wiggle room there is around a percentage.

            
📏
Plain-English version
A percentage from 347 students is much more stable than a percentage from 7 students. The confidence range reminds us not to overreact to small groups.

            

            
Rule of thumb: For groups below 20 students, do not treat the percentage as exact. Use it as a signal to investigate.

          

        


        

          

            
Pattern strength / r

            
The correlation number tells how tightly two things move together.

            

            

            
🔗
Teacher version
If r is near 0, the pattern is weak. If r is close to +1 or −1, the pattern is stronger. But a pattern is not proof that one thing caused the other.

            

              
Use r to…
  • Notice patterns worth discussing
  • Generate better questions
  • Compare signals across groups

              
Do not use r to…
  • Prove cause and effect
  • Make individual student decisions
  • Evaluate teachers or students

            

          

        


        

          

            
Chance check / p-value

            
The p-value asks whether a pattern is likely to be random noise.

            
🎲
Plain-English version
A small p-value means the pattern is unlikely to be random. It does not mean the pattern is large, important, or caused by the predictor.

            
Use this wording: “This pattern is unlikely to be random, but we still need teacher knowledge to understand why it is happening.”

          

        


        

          

            
Priority signal

            
A planning index that combines several risk signals into one number.

            
🚦
Like a dashboard warning light
Higher priority means more signals are showing at the same time: missed target, decline, LTML status, foundational score, or a larger gap. It tells the team where to look first.

            
This is not a label. Teacher knowledge and context should always override the score.

          

        


        

          

            
What this data cannot tell you

            
Using data well means being honest about its limits.

            

              
1. ACCESS is not academic ability
A student can be strong in math or science and still be developing English.

              
2. Small groups are unstable
A 100% result from 4 students is not the same as a 100% result from 40 students.

              
3. Correlation is not causation
A pattern can raise a question, but it cannot explain why the pattern happened by itself.
