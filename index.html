Statistics guide

Short, plain-language explanations designed to prevent misuse.

Read the data like a teacher, not a statistician first

Start with what the pattern suggests for instruction. Then ask how confident you should be, whether the group is large enough to trust, and whether there is a more likely classroom explanation.

What a percentage means

A percentage tells you the share of students in a group. It does not tell you whether the group is large or small, so always check the number of students beside it.

What growth means

Growth is the change in score from one year to the next. Growth can be positive and still fall short of the target.

What a gap means

The gap tells you how far a student is from the goal. A near miss means the gap is very small and may respond well to a short, targeted cycle.

What correlation means

Correlation describes whether two things tend to move together. It does not prove that one caused the other.

What small groups mean

Small groups are unstable. A result from 4 students can change a lot with just one different outcome, so treat it as a signal, not a final conclusion.

What to avoid

Do not use this dashboard to label students, prove cause and effect, or evaluate a teacher using one number alone.
